OKR Training - Instructor Guide

Complete Delivery Notes for 60-Minute Session

Use this guide alongside the presentation. It includes detailed talking points, stories, facilitation tips, and the workshop flow.

Pre-Session Setup (5 minutes before start)

  • Open presentation.html in browser, full screen
  • Have student notes ready to share/print
  • Have a whiteboard for live examples
  • Have a timer for workshop segment (15 minutes)
  • Test keyboard navigation (arrow keys, spacebar, 'n' for notes)

Section 1: Opening & Context (5 minutes)

Slide 1: Title

Your Opening (2 minutes)

Start with energy. This is NOT a boring productivity talk.

What to say:

"Today we're talking about the #1 thing that separates founders who 10x their business from those who grind for years with no progress: How they focus.

Not intelligence. Not luck. Not even hard work. It's focus.

Most founders fail because they're trying to do 20 things at once. By the end of today, you'll have a tool that lets you do 3-5 things exceptionally well.

That tool is called OKRs."

Slides 2-3: What & Why

Key Teaching Point

OKRs are not new. They're just a framework for what high performers have always done: decide what matters, and measure it.

Emphasize: "If you're afraid to fail, you're not going to make radical innovation."

This is Vishen's core insight. OKRs make failure safe.

💡 Tip: Tell a Story

Share a personal story about a time you chased too many things. What happened? How did focus change things?

Section 2: The F.A.C.T.S. Framework (15 minutes)

Slides 4-6: Framework Deep Dive

This is the backbone. Spend TIME here.

F - FOCUS

Say: "The hardest part of entrepreneurship is saying 'no'. Focus means 'We will do THESE 3-5 things this quarter, and we won't do anything else.'"

Example: Amazon started with books. Just books. Not electronics, clothing, furniture... for YEARS. That focus is why they won.

A - ALIGNMENT

Say: "Your CFO is chasing revenue. Your product team is chasing features. Your marketing team is chasing vanity metrics. Everyone's frustrated."

Example: When alignment works, magic happens. Everyone moves together. Decisions are easy because everyone knows the context.

C - COMMITMENT

Say: "You set these OKRs together. Your team commits. Then you don't change them mid-quarter because 'something exciting came up.'"

Key phrase: "Without commitment, OKRs are just wishful thinking."

T - TRACKING

Say: "You check in every week. 15 minutes. 'Are we on track? What's blocking us? Do we need to adjust our tactics?'"

Reality: This is where most companies fail. They set good OKRs, then lose visibility.

S - STRETCHING

Say: "You set OKRs where you're 70% confident you can achieve them."

Ask the room: "How many of you hit 100% of your goals last quarter?" (Most will raise hands)

"That means your goals aren't ambitious enough. Reset for next quarter."

💡 Interactive Moment

Ask: "What would change if you only set goals where you're 70% confident?"

Let them respond. You're building psychological ownership.

Section 3: Types & Mistakes (10 minutes)

Slides 7-9: OKR Types & Common Pitfalls

Committed vs. Aspirational

Say: "Most founders only set one type: easy goals they know they can hit.

But a healthy quarter has BOTH:

  • Committed: "We will hit $10M ARR" (90% confident)
  • Aspirational: "We will launch a new product line" (70% confident)

The right mix: 60% committed, 40% aspirational."

Common Mistakes

The #1 mistake I see: "Founders set 15 OKRs. Then wonder why nothing gets done.

FOCUS MEANS NO.

Set 3-5 company OKRs. Not 15. Not 10. 3-5."

Section 4: How to Set OKRs (10 minutes)

Slide 10: The 6-Step Process

Walk through this step-by-step. This is operational, so slow down.

Step 1
Define Core Purpose: What's your 1-3 year mission? Break it into aspirational objectives.
Step 2
Identify 3-5 Company OKRs: From your core purpose. Not 20. Focus on what moves the needle.
Step 3
Break into Key Results: For each objective, create 3-5 measurable key results.
Step 4
Cascade to Teams: Each team creates OKRs that support company goals (vertical alignment).
Step 5
Make Them Public: Everyone sees everyone's OKRs. Transparency drives accountability.
Step 6
Track Weekly: 15-min check-ins every Monday. No surprises.

Section 5: Workshop (15 minutes)

Slides 11-12: Hands-On OKR Building

Your Role

Give clear instructions (1 minute):

"Alright, your turn. You have 15 minutes to draft ONE OKR for your business.

I don't care if it's perfect. I care if it's real.

Use the template in the student notes:

  • Objective (1-2 sentences, inspirational)
  • 3 Key Results (specific, measurable, ambitious)

I'll walk around. Ask me questions. Let's make this real."

✅ During Workshop (Next 13 minutes)

Walk around the room. This is where real learning happens.

For each student, ask:

  • "What's your Objective?"
  • "How will you measure success?"
  • "Are you 70% confident or 95%?"

If vague: Challenge them: "'Improve marketing' — how? What metric? What's the target?"

If too easy: Push: "If you're 95% confident, bump it up. What if you 3x that target?"

💡 Pro Tip

Ask 2-3 students to share their draft during workshop. Praise what's good. Gently challenge what needs work. This public feedback teaches everyone.

Section 6: Tracking & Closing (5 minutes)

Slides 13-15: Closing Message

Recap

Quickly recap the 7 key takeaways. They'll remember these if you emphasize the 70% rule one more time.

Your Closing (2 minutes)

"Here's what I want you to do this week:

  1. Finalize ONE OKR for your business using what we covered today.
  2. Share it with me (optional, but I'll give feedback).
  3. Put it somewhere visible. Your bathroom mirror. Your desk. Your phone lock screen.
  4. Every Monday morning, ask: 'Are we on track?'

That's it. One OKR. One quarter. See what happens.

Most of you will 10x your business in the next 18 months because of this ONE tool. Not because you work harder. Because you work smarter.

Questions?"

Facilitation Tips

Energy Management

  • Start hot. Opening 5 minutes are crucial. Set the tone.
  • Vary pacing. Lecture 5 min, story 2 min, ask question 1 min. Mix it up.
  • Peak at workshop. The best part is hands-on. Don't rush.
  • Close strong. End with a challenge, not a summary.

Handling Questions

  • During slides: "Great question. Write it down. Let's discuss during workshop."
  • During workshop: "Exactly. That's the thinking that leads to good OKRs."
  • After: "Come to office hours. We'll dig deeper."

If Things Go Wrong

  • Running late on slides? Skip Slide 9 (common mistakes). You can cover in notes.
  • Workshop running long? That's fine. Lessons are learned hands-on.
  • Technical issues? Presentation works offline.

Follow-Up (After Session)

  • Send student notes link within 24 hours
  • Ask students to submit their ONE OKR draft by Friday
  • Provide 2-3 sentences of feedback on each
  • Offer office hours next week for deep dives

✅ Success Indicators

By end of session, students should be able to:

  • Define an OKR (Objective + Key Results)
  • Explain the 70% confidence rule
  • Identify committed vs. aspirational OKRs
  • Draft one OKR for their business
  • Explain why tracking weekly matters
